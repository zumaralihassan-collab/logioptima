Strategic insights

Mode choice is a value-vs-weight tradeoff
Trucks dominate by both value and weight domestically (~73% / ~65%) due to flexibility and last-mile reach. Pipelines and rail punch far above their value share on weight — they haul cheap, dense bulk (crude, coal, grain). Air is the inverse: <1% of weight, but a large share of value (electronics, pharma).
Crude oil is a pipeline-then-tanker game
Domestically crude moves cheapest by pipeline (~$5/bbl); rail (~$10–15) and truck (~$20) are 2–4x costlier and used only where pipe is unavailable. Internationally it shifts to marine tankers — the second-cheapest mode and the backbone of export.
Expedite = trade money for time
Air is ~10x ocean per kg but ~10x faster (3 vs 30 days). Expedited/express air adds another ~1.5–2.5x. The lever: ship only high-value or time-critical SKUs by air; everything else by the cheapest mode that meets the service window.
